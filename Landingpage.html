Heat Treatment Services

We offer a variety of heat treatment services, including quenching, tempering, annealing, and shot blasting. Our team of experts is dedicated to providing the highest quality service to meet your needs.

Quenching

Quenching is a process used to harden steel and other metals. It involves heating the metal to a high temperature and then rapidly cooling it by immersing it in a liquid, such as water or oil. This rapid cooling causes the metal to harden, making it suitable for use in applications that require high strength and wear resistance.

Tempering

Tempering is a process used to reduce the hardness of quenched steel and other metals. It involves reheating the metal to a lower temperature and then allowing it to cool slowly. This process reduces the internal stresses in the metal, making it less brittle and more ductile. The resulting metal has a combination of high strength and toughness, making it suitable for a wide range of applications.

Annealing

Annealing is a process used to soften steel and other metals. It involves heating the metal to a high temperature and then allowing it to cool slowly. This process reduces the internal stresses in the metal, making it more malleable and easier to work with. The resulting metal has a lower hardness and strength, making it suitable for applications that require good formability and machinability.

Shot Blasting

Shot blasting is a process used to clean, strengthen, or shape metal surfaces. It involves propelling small metal or mineral particles at high velocity onto the surface of the metal. This process removes rust, scale, and other contaminants from the surface, leaving a clean and textured finish. It can also be used to impart compressive stress on the surface, improving its fatigue resistance.
